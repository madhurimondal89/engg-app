About Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law is one of the most fundamental laws in electrical engineering, discovered by German physicist Georg Simon Ohm in 1827. It states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points.

Key Formulas:

  • V = I × R (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  • I = V / R (Current = Voltage / Resistance)
  • R = V / I (Resistance = Voltage / Current)
  • P = V × I (Power = Voltage × Current)
